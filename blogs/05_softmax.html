Flash Attention Series - Mastering Softmax

April 06, 2026 • 10 min read

Flash attention is currently one of the most important mainstream attention algorithm. Online softmax sits at the heart of the algorithm and understanding this will allow you to understand flash attention more smoothly.

Pytorch defines softmax as a function that is applied on a tensor of N dimension such that the output tensor is squished between [0, 1] and all the elements sum together to 1.

$$ \mathrm{softmax}(x_i) = \frac{e^{x_i}}{\sum_{j=1}^{n} e^{x_j}} $$

In this post, I'll walk through 4 variations of softmax, a naive version, safe softmax, online softmax and blocked softmax.

Naive Softmax

Well, if you had to guess just by looking at the formula, what would be the 3 major steps you need to compute softmax? 1. Numerator is simply the exponential of each element in the tensor. 2. Denominator is the sum of all of the exponential of each element in the tensor, or more simply the sum of numerator. 3. we then divide the numerator by the denominator. how easy, lets implement this now without invoking the functional softmax and do it by hand. 


    x = torch.tensor([0.2, 0.5, 0.1, -0.5, -0.4])
    
    # step 1. we take the sum of exp of x
    num = torch.exp(x)

    # step 2. we take the sum of exp of x
    denom = torch.sum(torch.exp(x))

    # step 3. we divide num/denom
    result = num/denom
    print(result)

Safe Softmax

Ow, but we forgot something, try and plug in a ridiculously large number in the tensor and take the exp of it. 


    print(torch.exp(torch.tensor(100)))

This gives you an infinity.

But remember for the softmax, we wont be having just a single number, we will be having multiple numbers in the tensor

What if we subtract the maximum of the entire tensor across each element of the tensor and then do softmax, the math indeed works out because [explain].

The formula would look something like this

$$ $$ 

    x = torch.tensor([0.2, 0.5, 0.1, -0.5, -0.4, 100])
    
    largest = torch.max(x)

    # subtract the largest from each value
    normalized_x = x - largest

    # do the same as before

    num = torch.exp(normalized_x)
    denom = torch.sum(torch.exp(normalized_x))
    result = num/denom
    print(result)

Online Softmax

Now that we understand softmax, we need to think deeply about two things -

1. we will be doing softmax in the gpu, so what happens if our tensor is like super long, so long that it doesnt fit into memory? 2. currently we need 3 passes, thats a lot of read and write to and from the HBM, can we make this faster?

Well, we could probably break it up, but we need to keep a track of the maximum and also the denominator as well, how can we do this?

Online softmax solves exactly this problem. Lets try to work this out step by step
